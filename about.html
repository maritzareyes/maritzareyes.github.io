I received my bachelor's degree in Geography/Environmental Studies and GIS from the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). It wasn't until my last two years in college I fell in love with Geography and GIS, and decided to pursue a career in it. During my time as an undergraduate, I worked under the head GIS/Geography Librarian at UCLA's main research library, Charles E. Young. I first helped in cataloging historical maps, and providing faculty and students with geographic/GIS data (image above is a historical map of Mexico City from 1749). However, I was later part of bigger GIS projects for the library. Some of these projects included publishing an interactive map about our map collections!

After obtaining my degree, I began working as a GIS intern for the Fullterton Arboretum where I assisted in mapping out all the vegetation. At the same time I was also working part-time at an oil consulting software development company called Earth Science Associates. We primarily focus on obtaining data for the Gulf of Mexico. My main duties have been maintaining and updating our automated pocesses for our GIS platform and desktop application. One current project we are working on is putting all our main data into a geodatabase.

Today, I continue to work full-time at Earth Science Associates, and am in the process of obtaining my Master's Degree in GIS & Web Map Programming from the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I am expected to graduate Fall 2019.
